About The Artists

Mendi and Keith Obadike's Legacy

Mendi and Keith Obadike's legacy is one of innovation, collaboration, and social impact. Their journey from New York City to the forefront of contemporary art is marked by a commitment to exploring the intersection of art, technology, and social change. Their work has not only earned them recognition on national and international stages but also sparked important dialogues about race, identity, and the role of art in society. As they look towards the future, Mendi and Keith remain dedicated to their artistic vision, eager to embark on new creative adventures and inspire others to push the boundaries of what is possible in the world of art and technology.

Impact on Net Art and Discussions About Race

In the nascent days of the internet, discussions about race were notably absent, and the realm of net art offered limited opportunities for Black voices to be heard. In response, Keith and Mendi Obadike's groundbreaking work emerges as a powerful force, redefining the internet as not just a repository of information but as a canvas for expressing the Black American experience. Their art provides a vital platform for exploring social issues and personal reflections, offering a beacon of expression in an otherwise barren digital landscape. Today, the influence of Black American culture pervades internet trends and vernacular, marking a significant shift from earlier times when such visibility was scarce. Through their pioneering efforts, the Obadikes have helped carve out a space where discussions about race flourish, contributing to a more inclusive and vibrant online community.
